Intercepts from an equation

Determine the intercepts of the line: $$y=-7x+3$$ y-intercept: $$y=3$$ x-intercept: $$7x+y=3$$ $$7x=3-y$$ $$x=\frac{3-y}7$$ $$x=\frac37$$

Determine the intercepts of the line: $$4x-3y=17$$ y-intercept: $$-3y=-4x+17$$ $$y=\frac{-4x+17}{-3}$$ $$y=-\frac{17}{3}$$ x-intercept: $$4x=3y+17$$ $$x=\frac{3y+17}{4}$$ $$x=\frac{17}{4}$$

Determine the intercepts of the line: $$5x-9=-8y-3$$ x-intercept: $$5x=-8y+6$$ $$x=\frac{-8y+6}{5}$$ $$x=\frac65$$ y-intercept: $$5x-6=-8y$$ $$\frac{5x-6}{-8}=y$$ $$\frac34=y$$

Determine the intercepts of the line: $$y=8x-18$$ y-intercept: $$y=8(0)-18$$ $$y=-18$$ x-intercept: $$0=8x-18$$ $$18=8x$$ $$\frac94=x$$

Determine the intercepts of the line:
$$-6x+3y=-7$$ x-intercept: $$-6x=-3y-7$$ $$x=\frac{-3y-7}{-6}$$ $$x=\frac{-3(0)-7}{-6}$$ $$x=\frac{7}{6}$$ y-intercept: $$3y=6x-7$$ $$y=\frac{6x-7}3$$ $$y=\frac{6(0)-7}3$$ $$y=-\frac{7}3$$
Determine the intercepts of the line:
$$y-4=7(x-6)$$ $$y-4=7x-42$$ $$y=7x-38$$ x-intercept: $$0=7x-38$$ $$38=7x$$ $$\frac{38}{7}=x$$ y-intercept: $$y=7(0) - 38$$ $$y=-38$$
Determine the intercepts of the line:
$$y=-9x - 14$$ To find the x-intercept, substitute y=0 into the equation and solve for x: $$0=-9x - 14$$ $$9x=-14$$ $$x=-\frac{14}{9}$$
$$2x+5y=-6$$ 

(defn round [precision d]
  (let [factor (Math/pow 10 precision)]
    (/ (Math/round (* d factor)) factor)))

; find y intercept:

(loop [x 0 y -100]
  (if (= (+ (* 2 x)
            (* 5 y))
         -6)
      y
 (recur x
   (round 1 (+ y 0.1)))))
Find x intercept: 

(loop [x -100 y 0]
  (if (= (+ (* 2 x)
            (* 5 y))
         -6)
      x
      (recur (round 1 (+ x 0.1))
             y)))
