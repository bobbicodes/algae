Solving proportions - "Rita's Pancakes"

EDIT: I'm pretty sure this is wrong! (It just happens to work w/ this prob) Chef Rita is cooking for a Sunday brunch. She knows that 22 pancakes can feed 8 people. She is wondering how many people (p) she can feed with 55 pancakes. She assumes each person eats the same quantity of pancakes.

How many people can Rita feed with 55 pancakes?

We're dealing with a proportional relationship, so each ratio of pancakes to people must be equivalent:
$$\dfrac{55\text{ pancakes}}{p \text{ people}} = \dfrac{22\text{ pancakes}}{8\text{ people}}$$
Live Clojure function: 

(defn pancakes [num-cakes will-feed num-people]
  (filter #(= (/ num-cakes %)
            (/ will-feed num-people))
        (range 100)))

(pancakes 55 22 8)
Solve by cross-multiplication:
$$\dfrac{55}{p } = \dfrac{22}{8} \\\\$$ Multiply both sides by 8: $$8\cdot\dfrac{55}{p } = \dfrac{22}{8} \cdot {8}$$ $$8\cdot \dfrac{55}{p} = 22$$ Multiply both sides by p: $$p \cdot 8\cdot \dfrac{55}{p} = 22 \cdot p$$ $$8 \cdot 55 = 22 \cdot p$$ $$440 = 22p$$ Divide both sides by 22: $$p = \dfrac{440}{22}$$ $$p=20$$ Rita can serve 20 people with 55 pancakes.
