Tax and tip word problems

Mia hired a moving company. The company charged $\$500$ for its services, and Mia gives the movers a $16\%$ tip.

How much does Mia tip the movers? 

(defn amount-tip [price percent-tip]
  (let [percent-as-decimal (/ percent-tip 100)]
    (* price percent-as-decimal)))

(amount-tip 500 16)


Mira has breakfast at a restaurant. She leaves a $20\%$ tip of $\$1.80$.

What is the price of Mira's breakfast, before tip? 

(defn price-before-tip [price-tip percent-tip]
  (let [percent-as-decimal (/ percent-tip 100)]
    (/ price-tip percent-as-decimal)))

(price-before-tip 1.80 20)


David is buying a cheese wheel priced at $\$650$ before tax. The store charges $8\%$ sales tax.

What is the total price, including tax, David pays for the cheese wheel? 

(defn total-price [price-before percent-tax]
  (let [percent-as-decimal (/ percent-tax 100)
        amount-tax (* percent-as-decimal price-before)]
    (+ price-before amount-tax)))
    
(total-price 650 8)

Equivalent percent expressions

Landen spent $L$ hours at the beach last weekend. Matéo spent $15\%$ fewer hours at the beach than Landen did.

The expressions $L(1-{0.15})$ and $L-{\dfrac{3}{20}}L$ each could represent how many hours Matéo spent at the beach last weekend.

After learning how to fly, Wendy reduced her daily commute time by $75\%$. Previously, her commute took $m$ minutes.

How can we represent Wendy's commute time after she learned how to fly?

Before learning how to fly, Wendy spent ${m}$ minutes on her commute daily. After learning how to fly, she spends $75\%$ less time commuting.

So Wendy spends ${75\%\cdot m}$ fewer minutes commuting daily than she used to.

That means Wendy spends ${m}-{75\% \cdot m}$ minutes commuting.

Because $75\%$ means $75$ per hundred, we can write it in equivalent forms: $$\begin{aligned} 75\%&=\dfrac{75}{100}\\\\ &=\dfrac{3}{4}\\\\ &=0.75 \end{aligned}$$ Equivalent ways of writing Wendy's commute time after she learned how to fly: $$\begin{aligned} m-{75\%} \cdot m&=m-{0.75}m\\\\ &=m(1-{0.75})\\\\ &=m(0.25)\\\\ &=0.25m \\\\ &=\dfrac{1}{4}m \end{aligned}$$

The expressions $0.25m$ and $\dfrac{1}{4}m$ each could represent Wendy's commute time, in minutes, after she learned how to fly.

After reading 80% of her e-mails in her inbox, Danette still has $M$ unread e-mails.

Danette started with 100% of her e-mails. After reading 80% of her e-mails, she has ${M}$ unread e-mails remaining.

So, ${M}$ represents $100\%-80\%={20\%}$ of the total number of e-mails.

That means the total number of e-mails is $\dfrac{{M}}{{20\%}}\cdot100\%$.

Because 20% means 20 per hundred, we can write it in equivalent forms. $$\begin{aligned} 20\%&=\dfrac{20}{100}\\\\ &=\dfrac{1}{5}\\\\ &=0.2 \end{aligned}$$ Let's find equivalent ways of writing the total number of e-mails. $$\begin{aligned} \dfrac{M}{{20\%}}\cdot 100\% &= \dfrac{M}{{0.2}}\cdot1 \\\\ &=\dfrac{M}{{1-0.8}} \\\\ &=\dfrac{M}{{\left(\dfrac{1}{5}\right)}} \\\\ &={5}M \end{aligned}$$

Eiko is wearing a magic ring that increases the power of her healing spell by 30%. Without the ring, her healing spell restores H health points.

The healing spell restores $30\%\cdot H$ more health points with the ring than without it.

That means the healing spell restores $30\%\cdot H+{H}$ health points with the ring.

Because 30% means 30 per hundred, we can write it in equivalent forms. $$\begin{aligned} 30\%&=\dfrac{30}{100}\\\\ &=\dfrac{3}{10}\\\\ &=0.3 \end{aligned}$$ $$\begin{aligned} {30\%}\cdot H+H&={\dfrac{3}{10}}H+H\\\\ &=\left({\dfrac{3}{10}}+1\right)H\\\\ &=\left({\dfrac{30}{100}}+1\right)H\\\\ &=\dfrac{130}{100}H \end{aligned}$$

In March, Delphine's house had 40% more snowfall than in February. Delphine's house had $f$ centimeters of snowfall in February.

Delphine's house had ${f}$ centimeters of snowfall in February, and there were 40% more centimeters of snowfall in March.

So there were ${40\%\cdot f}$ more centimeters of snowfall in March than in February.

That means her house had a total of ${40\% \cdot f} + {f}$ centimeters of snowfall in March.

Because 40% means 40 per hundred, we can write it in equivalent forms. $$\begin{aligned} 40\%&=\dfrac{40}{100}\\\\ &=\dfrac{4}{10}\\\\ &=0.4 \end{aligned}$$ Let's find equivalent ways of writing the amount of snowfall Delphine's house had in March. $$\begin{aligned} {40\%} \cdot f + f&={\dfrac{4}{10}}f+f\\\\ &= {0.4}f+f\\\\ &={0.4}f+1f\\\\ &=({0.4}+1)f\\\\ &=1.4f \end{aligned}$$

Part-part-whole ratios

Tara makes 30 cups of donut topping by mixing sugar and cinnamon. The ratio of sugar to cinnamon is 3:2.

How much sugar did Tara use in the donut topping?

The part to whole ratio of sugar to total is 3:5. 

(defn ratio [part whole total]
  (* part (/ total whole)))

(ratio 3 5 30)


(defn gcd [a b]
  (if (zero? b)
    a
    (recur b (mod a b))))

(defn rat [p w]
  [(/ p (gcd p w)) (/ w (gcd p w))])
