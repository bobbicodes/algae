Scientific notation word problems

A boy band has released two albums. Their first album sold $5.9 \cdot 10^5$ copies. Their second album sold $1.3 \cdot 10^6$ copies.

How many total copies has the boy band sold? 


(defn scinote [n]
  (loop [n n  acc 0]
     (if (< 10 n)
          (recur (/ n 10) (inc acc))
           (str n " x 10^" acc))))

(defn sci-add [s1 e1 s2 e2]
  (scinote (+ (* s1 (Math/pow 10 e1))
                   (* s2 (Math/pow 10 e2)))))

(sci-add 5.9 5 1.3 6)

Multiplying & dividing in scientific notation

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{1.88 \times 10^6}{2000}} =\ ?$$ $$\large{\dfrac{1.88 \times 10^6}{2 \times 10^{-3}}}$$ $$\large{0.94 \times 10^{6 \,-\, -3}}$$ $$\large{0.94 \times 10^{6 \,-\, -3}}$$ $$\large{1.3 \times 10^{-2}}$$

Calculate product in scientific notation. $$\large{(1.3 \times 10^{-2}) \times (0.05) =\ ?}$$ $$\large{(1.3 \times 10^{-2}) \times (5 \times {10^{-2}})}$$ $$\large{18.8 \times {10^{2 \,+\, 3}}}$$ $$\large{1.88 \times {10^{6}}}$$

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{0.00003}{2 \times 10^{-5}}} =\ ?$$ $$\large{\dfrac{3}{2}} \times {\dfrac{10^{-5}}{10^{-5}}}$$ $$= 1.3 \times 10^{-4 \,-\, -2}$$ $$= 1.3 \times 10^{-2}$$

Calculate product in scientific notation. $$\large{\left(8\times 10^{-3} \right) \times (0.0002) =\ ?}$$ $$\large{(8 \times 2) \times ({10^{-3}} \times {10^{-4}})}$$ $$\large{16 \times {10^{-3 \,+\, -4}}}$$ $$\large{16 \times {10^{-7}}}$$ $$\large{1.6 \times {10^{-6}}}$$

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{0.00065}{5 \times 10^{-2}}} =\ ?$$ $$\large{\dfrac{6.5}{5}} \times {\dfrac{10^{-4}}{10^{-2}}}$$ $$= 1.3 \times 10^{-4 \,-\, -2}$$ $$= 1.3 \times 10^{-2}$$

Calculate product in scientific notation. $$\large{\left(8.2\times 10^{-3} \right) \times \left(2.0\times 10^{2} \right) =\ ?}$$ $$\large{(8.2 \times 2.0) \times ({10^{-3}} \times {10^{2}})}$$ $$\large{16.4 \times {10^{-3 \,+\, 2}}}$$ $$\large{1.64 \times {10^{0}}}$$

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{1.72 \times 10^{0}}{4.0 \times 10^{-3}}} =\ ?$$ $$\large{\dfrac{1.72}{4.0}} \times {\dfrac{10^{0}}{10^{-3}}}$$ $$= 0.43 \times 10^{0 \,-\, -3}$$ $$= 0.43 \times 10^{3}$$ $$= 4.3 \times 10^2$$

Calculate product in scientific notation. $$\large{\left(3.0\times 10^{3} \right) \times \left(5.0\times 10^{1} \right) =\ ?}$$ $$\large{(3.0 \times 5.0) \times ({10^{3}} \times {10^{1}})}$$ $$\large{15 \times {10^{3 \,+\, 1}}}$$ $$\large{1.5 \times {10^{5}}}$$

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{3.8\times 10^{9}}{400}} =\ ?$$ $$\large{\dfrac{3.8}{4.0}} \times {\dfrac{10^{9}}{10^{2}}}$$ $$= {0.95} \times {10^{9 \,-\, 2}}$$ $$= 0.95 \times 10^{7}$$ $$= 9.5 \times 10^6$$

Calculate product in scientific notation. $$\large{\left(5.2\times 10^{-2} \right) \times \left(8.0\times 10^{-4} \right) =\ ?}$$ $$\large{(5.2 \times 8.0) \times ({10^{-2}} \times {10^{-4}})}$$ $$\large{41.6 \times {10^{-2 \,+\, -4}}}$$ $$\large{41.6 \times {10^{-6}}}$$ $$\large{4.16 \times {10^{-5}}}$$

Calculate quotient in scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{2.85\times 10^{6}}{30,000}} =\ ?$$ Collect the significands and exponents. $$\large{\dfrac{2.85}{3.0}} \times {\dfrac{10^{6}}{10^{4}}}$$ Then divide each term separately. When dividing exponents with the same base, subtract their powers. $$= {0.95} \times {10^{6 \,-\, 4}}$$ $$= 0.95 \times 10^{2}$$ $$= 9.5 \times 10^1$$

Calculate product using scientific notation. $$\large{\left(1.5\times 10^{0} \right) \times \left(2.0\times 10^{-5} \right) =\ ?}$$ Collect significands and exponents: $$\large{(1.5 \times 2.0) \times ({10^{0}} \times {10^{-5}})}$$ Multiply each term separately. When multiplying exponents with the same base, add the powers together. $$\large{3 \times {10^{0 \,+\, -5}}}$$ $$\large{3 \times {10^{-5}}}$$

Calculate product using scientific notation. $$\large{\left(3.3\times 10^{-4} \right) \times \left(8.0\times 10^{0} \right) =\ ?}$$ Collect the significands and exponents: $$\large{(3.3 \times 8.0) \times ({10^{-4}} \times {10^{0}})}$$ Then multiply each term separately. When multiplying exponents with the same base, add the powers together. $$= 26.4 \times {10^{-4 \,+\, 0}}$$ $$= 26.4 \times {10^{-4}}$$ To write the answer correctly in scientific notation, the first number needs to be between 1 and 10. In this case, we need to move the decimal one position to the left without changing the value of our answer.

We can use the fact that $26.4$ is the same as $2.64 \times 10$ or $2.64 \times 10^{1}$. $$= 2.64 \times 10^{1} \times {10^{-4}}$$ $$= 2.64 \times 10^1 + -4$$ $$= 2.64\times 10^{-3}$$

Calculate quotient. Use scientific notation. $$\large{\dfrac{7.80\times 10^{7}}{1.0\times 10^{4}}} =\ ?$$ Collect the significands and exponents. $$\large{\dfrac {{7.80} \times {10^{7}}} {{1.0} \times {10^{4}}} = {\dfrac{7.80}{1.0}} \times {\dfrac{10^{7}}{10^{4}}}}$$ Then divide each term separately. When dividing exponents with the same base, subtract their powers. $$= {7.80} \times {10^{7 \,-\, 4}}$$ $$= 7.80 \times 10^{3}$$
